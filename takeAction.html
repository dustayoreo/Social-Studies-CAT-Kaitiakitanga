Take Action

Environmental issues like recycling contamination and product waste affect everyone. Here are a few solutions that can help reduce the environmental impact.

Individual Action

Reduce waste by following the 5 R's: Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, Repair, and Recycle. Make choices by avoiding unecessary products and choosing high quality or durable products.

Have app developers or use an app that can scan an item in your hand, telling you if it is recyclable or not — with at least an 80% accuracy, and it is easy to use, so that people with busy lives can easily use it without any trouble or wasting time. This can dramatically decrease the amount of recycling contamination.

Local Action

Support community repair places, recycling education programmes, and local clean-up events. Communities can work together to reduce contamination and improve recycling habits.

On a local scale, we could get volunteers(or pay them) to work at cafes or shops. These are repair shops, where locals can walk in and get their things repaired for free or at a low price.

National Action

Have an official Government stamp that every company that produces products must have stamped. This prevents greenwashing, as if the product has an official green stamp, consumers can easily tell if the product is environmentally friendly, recyclable, or not. Green means completely trustworthy and safe to buy. Orange means partially recyclable, and red means unsafe for the environment.
