Government Action

The New Zealand Government has introduced severl policies to reduce waste, improve recycling systems, and encourage more sustainable practices. However, it is heavily questioned if these solutions will go far enough and if it will be effective, as well as the fact that the Government is going as far to undoing the mining for fossil fuels ban - creating a conflict of interest.

Waste Strategy

Thee Government has released a new Waste Strategy and Waste Work Programme to reduce landfill waste and improve recycling.

Managing Contamination

Councils continue to encouage and spread awareness about cleaning recyclable materials and preventing contamination.

Current Debates

Stronger solutions and alternatives are needed, while the Government has stated that working with businesses and communities will improve the waste management over time.

Reversing Environmental Policies

The National-led Government has reversed or changed several environmental policies introduced by the previous government - the Labour Party, including ending New Zealand' inclusion in the Beyond Oil and Gas alliance. Such decisions as these will slow our progress to reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

Politicial Donations

Some companies involved in fossil fuels and construction industries have made politicial donations to parties, including National. The National Party has accepted these donations and granted great benifits to these companies such as GMP.

Waste Being Exported

New Zealand still exports some recyclable materials overseas, to poorer countries that have to deal with the consequences due to our actions. The contaminated waste can become another country's problem and raises questions to due with our environment responsibility.

Working With Businesses

The Government has stated that it wants to work with businesses, councils, and communities to improve our waste management.
