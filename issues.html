New Zealand's Environmental Issues

-Recycling Contamination-

  • One of the major causes of recycling contamination is people placing non-recyclable rubbish into recycling bins or not cleaning recyclable items before disposal. Food residues, liquids, and mixed substances can contaminate entire loads of recycling, making it either extremely difficult or impossible to process.

  • Government websites mainly focus on the benefits of recycling, although they also acknowledge challenges such as contamination and difficulties in processing some materials.

-Greenwashing-

  • Some companies label their products as ”recyclable” even when, in reality, recycling facilities cannot process them. This can make consumers believe they are disposing of waste when the packaging may actually end up in a landfill and spread its toxins to the surrounding environment. This makes customers believe they are doing the right thing, and this false packaging scheme is called "greenwashing."

    Greenwashing reduces the public's trust and makes it harder for genuinely sustainable businesses to make profit or compete. Greenwashing additionally encourages consumption for consumers, as they believe they are doing the right thing and use these products excessively.

-Product Waste-

  • Consumers constantly buy from cheap brands like Temu, Shein, and AliExpress because they provide cheap and affordable clothing, items, or products. Unfortunately, these products are designed to have a limited lifespan and wear out quickly — forcing you to buy their products again and again instead of buying one at a fairly reasonable price that is higher quality and lasts longer. A large factor that contributes to this topic is social media, used as a marketing tool for big companies and trends to sway people into buying things. This increases the demand for products and waste. It also uses fossil fuels to create these products, which fluctuate in price and waste. Therefore, consumerism encourages product waste and fossil fuel consumption.

  • When some companies use excessive packaging, are single-use and designed with a limited use-time/lifespan, so that consumers are forced into buying more of their products, letting the old, broken products be discarded. Businesses prioritise convenience and lower cost rather than the sustainability of their products.

-Landfills and Donors-

  • The National Party — ever since coming to government — has been undoing all of the fossil fuel bans that have previously been laid out to protect our environment. They are making Auckland ratepayers pay an extra $3 million per year so the council can address the contamination. The government is accepting large donations of up to $100k and more from companies that produce oil, gas, and fossil fuels, which further pollute our environment. In return, the National Party has been lifting the international fossil fuel pledge originally put in place by the Labour Party, along with many other bans. With this, we can argue that these policies appear inconsistent with the government’s stated goals of improving environmental outcomes. When waste is thrown into landfills, it breaks down into greenhouse gases and produces a toxic liquid called Leachate that mainly seeps into water sources.

-Consequences-

  • Increased amount of waste in landfills.
  • More greenhouse gases are being released from waste composting and the production of new materials or products because of overconsumption — this mostly happens when trends become popular on social media, and a bunch of products are wasted.
  • Increasing costs for councils and recycling facilities due to contamination.
  • Consumer confusion due to the misleading information on the packaging.
  • Consumer confusion due to whether or not something belongs in recycling bins.
  • Public concerns about whether the government’s decisions are in the best interest of New Zealand’s economy and environment, rather than for the power and wealth of businesses.
  • Environmental damage to ecosystems and wildlife due to pollution, and when landfill waste becomes toxic before seeping into its surroundings and killing them.
