New Zealand's Environmental Issues

-Recycling Contamination-

  • One of the major causes of recycling contamination is people placing non-recyclable rubbish into recycling bins or not cleaning recyclable items before disposal. Food residues, liquids, and mixed substances can contaminate entire loads of recycling, making it either extremely difficult or impossible to process.

  • Government websites mainly focus on the benefits of recycling, although they also acknowledge challenges such as contamination and difficulties in processing some materials.

  • Another cause is confusing packaging. Some companies label their products as ”recyclable” even when, in reality, recycling facilities cannot process them. This can make consumers believe they are disposing of waste when the packaging may actually end up in a landfill and spread its toxins to the surrounding environment.

  • Wastage can also be caused when some companies use excessive packaging, are single-use and designed with a limited use-time/lifespan, so that consumers are forced into buying more of their products, letting the old, broken products be discarded. Businesses prioritise convenience and lower cost rather than the sustainability of their products.
